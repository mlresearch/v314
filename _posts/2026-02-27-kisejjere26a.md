abstract: Despite recent advances in large language models, most of Uganda’s over
  40 indigenous languages remain underrepresented in natural language processing systems.
  This work introduces ugGPT, an open instruction-tuned model designed specifically
  for Luganda, spoken by over 20 million people. We also release a 200 million token
  monolingual Luganda corpus and a culturally contextualized instruction dataset with
  over 70,000 examples. In addition, we present accent-aware text-to-speech models
  for English, Luganda, Runyankole, Acholi, and Iteso, fine-tuned from the Orpheus
  3B architecture. Experimental results show that ugGPT outperforms multilingual baselines
  and that the speech models generate intelligible and natural audio for low-resource
  languages.
